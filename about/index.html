About.

Hello!

My name is Max Johnson, and I am a PhD student at the University of California, San Diego. My primary advisor is Zhouli Xu (UCSD) and I am co-advised by Robert Burklund (KU).

Interests

My interests generally fall under the umbrella of stable homotopy theory, both computational and theoretical. I am particularly interested in chromatic homotopy theory and its many intersections with algebraic/arithmetic geometry. My work-in-progress mostly concerns the study of "deformations" of stable infinity categories including synthetic spectra, Voevodsky's motivic homotopy category over the complex numbers, and the general theory of 1-parameter deformations as in the appendices of the Galois Reconstruction paper.
